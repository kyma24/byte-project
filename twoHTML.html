method 1: convert to base ten.
take the place value(i.e. two zero = one, two one = two, third = four, fourth = eight) of each digit in the base two number, then multiply it by the value of the digit(either one or zero). Add the results together, then convert back to base two(find the greatest number that is a power of two less than the base ten number. take the power number and make that a place value. the place value in base two will be one. next, subtract the number from the total base ten number. do the same with that remainder.) 		method 2: do it straight on.
put the two numbers in up and down form. add like with base ten, except when there is a one plus one, treat the result as ten and put down zero and carry down one. If there is a one plus one plus a carry on of one, same thing except put down one instead of zero.
- subtraction -
method 1: convert to base ten.
take the place value(i.e. two zero = one, two one = two, third = four, fourth = eight) of each digit in the base two number, then multiply it by the value of the digit(either one or zero). Subtract the results, then convert back to base two(find the greatest number that is a power of two less than the base ten number. take the power number and make that a place value. the place value in base two will be one. next, subtract the number from the total base ten number. do the same with that remainder.) 		method 2: do it straight on.
put the two numbers in up and down form. subtract like with base ten, except when carrying, keep in mind that when borrowing over a span of more than one digits, the passing of the one will create a ten, that equals a two in base ten. so during the repetition of borrowing, the trail of affected digits will be left with a one on top after borrowing over, and not a two or ten.
